On many academic institutions, the most frequently occurring items are Current Students, Prospective Students, Faculty and Staff, and Alumnus + Visitors. The least occurring item on many school websites on many of these sites main navigation system on the home page Athletics and Sports. This seem pretty obvious as although many campus activities such as Sports create some impact on the marketability of specific user groups, these groups do not cater to the larger and more frequently categories that either Current Students, Prospective Students, Faculty and Staff, and Alumnus + Visitors can encompass.

In addition to the main navigation system prescribed by the website, a most common feature of many academic institutions include a utility bar/quick links bar. Ultimately, like the websites, I agree a quick links bar is necessary because University transactions should not create time inefficiency but rather time efficiency. Ultimately, Current Students, Prospective Students, Faculty and Staff, and Alumnus + Visitors are the four most frequently used user groups.

Hardest Decisisons

The hardest decision was actually trying to figure out the most important aspect of user designs. Website linkage is important; however, the time it will take a user to find items should be minimum if organized right. User transaction should be quick and fast, thus a website needs to provide a way to to provide that need without compromising the integrity of information. Proper organizations in the right category can expediate users in their tranactions quicker and faster without clutter be evident.

For the most, I think my navigation system caters for the various users presented in my user scenarios. Students can access grades quicker through ULink being on the first page as well as also accessing Moddle - a program similar to Blackboard that is more highly and thouroughly used than here at UCSD. Donations from alumnis are quicker now and prospective donors can be captured more easily. Prospective students will notice the myraid of information and the eye please design to draw further attention into UL Lafayette. Unfortunately, I reluctantly put atheletics into my design because of my previous team members who chose it thought it was important. However, when they left me to do it alone, the design was already finished and there was no other alternative method to fix something that has been finished without rethinking new ways to find that new and better solution. Overall, I believe my design caters to the users efficently as possible - because in the fast-paced society of today, users want things done quickly and not slowly.
